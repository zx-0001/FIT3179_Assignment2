Socio-economic factors of different regions

With advances in science and technology, we are currently living in the most prosperous era in human history and this is especially true for those living in high-income countries. However, it is worth looking back and seeing exactly how much we have progressed as a whole on a global scale, to see how much progress has been achieved in different regions.

The following visualisations used data from the World Bank of different countriesâ€™ socio-economic variables and life expectancy from 2000-2019 and will be showing the differences between countries of different income groups.

The donut chart provides an overview of the proportions of the different income groups. From the data, we can see that over half of the countries are in the upper-middle and high-income classes in 2019.

The grouped bar charts above show the count of the income groups in each region in 2019. More details can be seen by hovering over the bars.

The main findings are that most high-income countries are in Europe & Central Asia while Sub-Saharan Africa has the highest number of low-income countries. This shows a clear inequality in income across regions

Intuitively speaking, more spending on health should increase life expectancy but from the graph, there does not appear to be any universal correlation between health expenditure (% GDP) and life expectancy. The legend can be clicked to highlight the region of interest.

Interestingly, some countries like Singapore, have high life expectancy despite having a lower % of health expenditure whereas a country like Afghanistan has a comparatively low life expectancy. This can either mean there are other more important unobserved variables that affect life expectancy or % of health expenditure is not a good variable to correlate with life expectancy.
